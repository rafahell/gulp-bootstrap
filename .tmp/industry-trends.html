GENERAL INDUSTRIES TRENDS

Chemicals and Natural Resources industries are still struggling with market headwinds and significant uncertainty.


Global Economy

Increasing market volatility, changing global demand and overcapacities.

Weak growth and downgrade in global economic outlook.

Divergence between advanced and emerging economies remains.


World events

Continuous tensions Middle East.

Natural disasters – number has more than doubled since 1980.


Energy prices

Volatile oil prices in general and low gas prices in North America.

Reduction of energy input and increasing energy efficiency.

Competitive economics varying between regions.


M&A / Divestments

Weak growth in traditional markets and product portfolios - search for additional growth potentials.

Realignment of strategic assets and divestments in non-core/ loss-making assets.

Productivity and working capital improvements.


Customer Engagement

Increasing spread between ever changing customer demands and the companies’ responsiveness.

Changing customer behavior - decline in customer loyalty and engagement.

Lack of sufficient customer insights – partially slow alignment to customer needs


Talent squeeze

Knowledge resides with a narrowing and aging workforce.

Radical innovations to drive sustainable competitiveness depend on talent.

Traditional CNR industries not the first preference of graduates.


Government regulations

Changes in regulatory environment.

Increasing tax rates.

Anti-dumping cases and contract reviews – potential threat of revoking licenses.


Digitalization

Traditional end-to-end business models are challenged by digitalization.

High Performers in CNR industry score well on digitalization initiatives.

Data integrity is imperative to enable effective decisions and improve productivity.


Sustainable practices

Increased focus on sustainability – sustainability investments on the rise.

Focus on energy efficiency and green products.

Increasing safety culture and continuous improvement.
